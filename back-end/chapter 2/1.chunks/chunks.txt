When a client sends data (like in a POST request), Node.js does NOT receive the whole request body at once.

that is If the client sends a 5 MB file, Node does not load all 5 MB into memory at once.

Instead, it receive the data in small small chunk(packet)

Client
   ↓
Request Body
   ↓
Chunk 1
Chunk 2
Chunk 3
Chunk 4
  .
  .
  .
chunk N  



1️⃣ What does a chunk look like?
{ <Buffer 6e 61 6d 65 3d 73 68 61 68 69 64> }


2️⃣ when you are seeing only one chunk??
If the request body is very small, maybe Node.js receives only one chunk
Client
   ↓
Request Body
   ↓         
Chunk 1  <Buffer 6e 61 6d 65>

3️⃣If the request body is big, nodejs might get multiple chunks:
Client
   ↓
Request Body
   ↓         
Chunk 1  <Buffer 6e 61 6d 65>
Chunk 2  <Buffer 6e 72 6d 65>
Chunk 3  <Buffer 6e 81 6d 65>
Chunk 4  <Buffer 6e 55 7d 65>
