Exploring The Alps

This card features a beautiful mountain background image with scrollable content area. The background image is fixed while the text content scrolls over it.

The Alps are the highest and most extensive mountain range system that lies entirely in Europe, stretching approximately 1,200 kilometers across eight Alpine countries.

key features

  • Highest peak: Mont Blanc (4,808 m)

  • Home to about 13,000 plant species

  • Contains more than 30,000 animal species

  • Major tourist destination for skiing and mountaineering

  • Source of many major European rivers

The Alpine region has a strong cultural identity. Traditional practices such as farming, cheesemaking, and woodworking still thrive in Alpine villages.

Climate change has had a significant impact on the Alps. Glaciers have been retreating since the end of the Little Ice Age in the mid-19th century, and the rate of retreat has increased dramatically since the 1980s.

popular activity :

  • Skiing and snowboarding in winter

  • Hiking and mountain biking in summer

  • Rock climbing and mountaineering

  • Paragliding and hang gliding

  • Visiting traditional Alpine villages

Whether you're an experienced mountaineer or just looking for a peaceful retreat, the Alps offer something for everyone. The combination of breathtaking scenery, rich culture, and diverse activities makes this region one of Europe's most cherished natural treasures.
